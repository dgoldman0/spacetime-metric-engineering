# Component Design Source PDFs

Status: local source index for physical-construction hypotheses.

These PDFs were downloaded for the component-design track. They are used as
physical analogs and constraint sources, not as evidence that the active rail is
already buildable.

## Analogue Gravity And Optical Geometry

- [`barcelo_liberati_visser_analogue_gravity_gr-qc0505065.pdf`](barcelo_liberati_visser_analogue_gravity_gr-qc0505065.pdf)  
  Carlos Barcelo, Stefano Liberati, Matt Visser, "Analogue Gravity,"
  arXiv:gr-qc/0505065. Used for the general discipline of emulating effective
  metrics in fluids, condensates, optics, and other media.

- [`leonhardt_philbin_transformation_optics_0805.4778.pdf`](leonhardt_philbin_transformation_optics_0805.4778.pdf)  
  Ulf Leonhardt and Thomas G. Philbin, "Transformation Optics and the Geometry
  of Light," arXiv:0805.4778. Used for the idea that material parameter fields
  can emulate optical geometry.

- [`philbin_fiber_optical_event_horizon_0711.4796.pdf`](philbin_fiber_optical_event_horizon_0711.4796.pdf)  
  Thomas G. Philbin et al., "Fiber-optical analogue of the event horizon,"
  arXiv:0711.4796. Used for actual laboratory horizon/collar analog hardware
  using pulses in microstructured optical fiber.

- [`greenleaf_electromagnetic_wormholes_metamaterials_140836.pdf`](greenleaf_electromagnetic_wormholes_metamaterials_140836.pdf)  
  Allan Greenleaf et al., "Electromagnetic wormholes and virtual magnetic
  monopoles from metamaterials," open-access PRL author version. Used for
  transformation-optics tunnels and invisible-cable style support analogs.

- [`smolyaninov_metamaterial_alcubierre_1009.5663.pdf`](smolyaninov_metamaterial_alcubierre_1009.5663.pdf)  
  Igor I. Smolyaninov, "Metamaterial-based model of the Alcubierre warp
  drive," arXiv:1009.5663. Used mainly as a cautionary analogue: metamaterial
  models can emulate some effective geometry, but available material parameters
  sharply limit the emulation.

## Source-Medium And Support-Reservoir Analogs

- [`denicol_heat_flow_transient_relativistic_fluid_1207.6811.pdf`](denicol_heat_flow_transient_relativistic_fluid_1207.6811.pdf)  
  G. S. Denicol et al., "Solving the heat-flow problem with transient
  relativistic fluid dynamics," arXiv:1207.6811. Used for causal/stable
  heat-flow variables and relaxation dynamics.

- [`eling_jacobson_mattingly_einstein_aether_gr-qc0410001.pdf`](eling_jacobson_mattingly_einstein_aether_gr-qc0410001.pdf)  
  C. Eling, T. Jacobson, D. Mattingly, "Einstein-Aether Theory,"
  arXiv:gr-qc/0410001. Used as a director-field analogy, not as a claim that
  the active rail is an Einstein-aether model.

- [`natario_rigid_elastic_solids_relativity_1912.08221.pdf`](natario_rigid_elastic_solids_relativity_1912.08221.pdf)  
  Jose Natario, "Rigid elastic solids in relativity," arXiv:1912.08221. Used
  for support-reservoir/elastic-stress intuition and for the reminder that
  relativistic support media must remain deformable and causal.

- [`wilson_dynamical_casimir_superconducting_circuit_1105.4714.pdf`](wilson_dynamical_casimir_superconducting_circuit_1105.4714.pdf)  
  C. M. Wilson et al., "Observation of the Dynamical Casimir Effect in a
  Superconducting Circuit," arXiv:1105.4714. Used as a real hardware example of
  fast effective-boundary modulation in superconducting circuits.

## Negative-Energy And Feasibility Limits

- [`ford_roman_qft_constrains_wormholes_gr-qc9510071.pdf`](ford_roman_qft_constrains_wormholes_gr-qc9510071.pdf)  
  L. H. Ford and Thomas A. Roman, "Quantum Field Theory Constrains Traversable
  Wormhole Geometries," arXiv:gr-qc/9510071. Used as a quantum-inequality
  warning for macroscopic exotic support.

- [`pfenning_ford_quantum_inequality_curved_spacetimes_gr-qc9805037.pdf`](pfenning_ford_quantum_inequality_curved_spacetimes_gr-qc9805037.pdf)  
  Michael J. Pfenning and L. H. Ford, "Quantum Inequality Restrictions on
  Negative Energy Densities in Curved Spacetimes," arXiv:gr-qc/9805037. Used
  for negative-energy magnitude/duration constraints in curved-spacetime
  settings.

- [`pfenning_ford_unphysical_warp_drive_gr-qc9702026.pdf`](pfenning_ford_unphysical_warp_drive_gr-qc9702026.pdf)  
  Michael J. Pfenning and L. H. Ford, "The unphysical nature of 'Warp Drive',"
  arXiv:gr-qc/9702026. Used as a cautionary limit on warp-drive wall thickness
  and negative-energy requirements.
