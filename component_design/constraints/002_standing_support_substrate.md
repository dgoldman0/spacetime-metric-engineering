## Role

The standing support substrate is the prepared railbed: throat/core support,
radial support shell, angular jacket, baseline lapse structure, and the
dominant non-live radial support body. It supplies the infrastructure that lets
the live packet avoid carrying the main source burden.

## Actual Physical Construction Hypothesis

The first honest construction guess is a permanent stressed scaffold, not a
drive engine. The closest physical analogs are:

- transformation-optics tunnels, where material parameters create an effective
  guide geometry for fields;
- tensegrity/elastic lattices, where load is carried by organized tension and
  compression rather than by a homogeneous block;
- relativistic-elastic or high-stiffness continuum models, used as mathematical
  analogs for causal stress storage;
- superconducting or photonic metamaterial arrays whose effective index,
  impedance, and anisotropy can be spatially patterned.

At lab scale, this suggests building the substrate as a fixed annular
metamaterial rail: an inner quiet bore, a radial stack of anisotropic cells,
and an outer return/stabilization shell. The "radial string-cloud" should be
translated physically as a distributed axial/radial tension network with nearly
constant flux per area, not as literal cosmic strings.

At speculative full scale, the substrate would be a pre-energized rail segment:

- radial-tension members or field channels carry the dominant `rho ~= -p_l`
  support;
- angular-capacity rings carry transverse/throat support;
- gradient-shaping layers smooth transitions into support-edge and endpoint
  regions;
- no service is armed until this standing plant is already in its ready state.

## Hardware Sketch

- Inner mechanical/field isolation bore.
- Radial backbone layer: repeated anisotropic tension cells or field-stress
  channels with nearly constant areal support density.
- Angular jacket: circumferential rings or metamaterial cells supplying
  transverse capacity.
- Outer reference shell: mechanically and thermally stabilizes the active
  support layers.
- Endpoint anchors: couple the standing substrate to receiver/reset hardware.

## Mathematical Constraints Carried Over

- The dominant non-live radial infrastructure body is close to
  NEC-saturating radial tension, with `p_l/rho` near `-1`.
- The areal flux proxy should stay nearly constant across the main radial body.
- The scaffold should remain non-live.
- Residual selected-null burden after subtracting the scaffold must be assigned
  to endpoint/support completion rather than ignored.
- Finite-domain radial ANEC remains a source-completion target, not a current
  pass claim.

## Design Implications

The scaffold has to be designed as a preconditioned infrastructure element. It
should be boring during service: no sharp transient, no passenger-region
activation, no singular edge layer. The support substrate is where a future
matter-action derivation will either earn credibility or fail, because
Ford-Roman/Pfenning-Ford style limits warn that macroscopic exotic support
cannot be hidden in arbitrarily thin negative-energy bands.
