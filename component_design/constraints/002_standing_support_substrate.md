## Role

The standing support substrate is the prepared railbed: throat/core support,
radial support shell, angular jacket, baseline lapse structure, and dominant
non-live radial support body. It supplies the standing infrastructure that lets
the live corridor remain a protected guide.

## Physical Construction Hypothesis

The substrate is a preloaded architected backbone. Its physical form is closest
to a civil-scale mechanical metamaterial: repeated cells, radial load paths,
angular rings, active shunts, and reservoir interfaces arranged so the rail has
a ready support state before a packet enters.

The radial string-cloud result maps to an areal support-density target. In
physical terms, that points to a graded lattice or field-stress network whose
cell density, preload, and anisotropy keep radial support nearly constant per
area through the main body. Flexible and programmable mechanical metamaterials
provide the relevant construction vocabulary: unit-cell geometry controls
effective stiffness, hysteresis, multistability, and shape response. Active
elastic waveguides and piezoelectric shunts add the service-time feature:
selected segments can alter mechanical impedance or wave routing under
electronic control.

## Candidate Physical Stack

- Inner carrier frame: precision alignment structure around the live bore.
- Radial backbone cells: prestressed tension/compression members or field
  channels graded by radius to match the areal flux proxy.
- Angular jacket: circumferential rings carrying transverse/throat capacity.
- Active shunt layer: piezoelectric patches, digital shunts, or active elastic
  cells that tune band gaps and suppress support-wave leakage.
- Energy/stress reservoir ports: mechanical, magnetic, or thermal storage
  interfaces that hand endpoint/support exchange to card `006`.
- Outer reference shell: civil-scale structure for thermal stability,
  alignment, service access, and sensor anchoring.

## Mathematical Constraints Carried Over

- The dominant non-live radial infrastructure body is close to
  NEC-saturating radial tension, with `p_l/rho` near `-1`.
- The areal flux proxy stays nearly constant across the main radial body.
- The scaffold remains assigned to non-live infrastructure.
- Residual selected-null burden after subtracting the scaffold is assigned to
  endpoint/support completion.
- Finite-domain radial ANEC remains a source-completion target.

## Design Implications

The substrate is built as a ready-state plant. It is measured before arming:
preload, anisotropy, temperature, shunt state, stress-wave leakage, and
endpoint anchor load must all sit inside the certified envelope. The physical
credibility of the radial backbone depends on distributing the support over
finite cell depth and measurable reservoir interfaces, since thin high-contrast
bands trigger the same energy-condition and boundedness concerns that appear
in the source diagnostics.
