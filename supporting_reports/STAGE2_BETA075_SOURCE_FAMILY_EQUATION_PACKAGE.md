4. finite internal angular-response closure;

6. two-channel support exchange, split into power `P` and radial force `F`;
7. conservation-restoring support tensor;
8. phase-local bounded source response;
9. service-coordinate scheduled release;
10. positive split-step rapidity transport;

The central conservation statement is:

```text
nabla_mu T_endpoint^{mu nu} + J_support^nu = 0
J_support^nu = P u^nu + F s^nu + J_perp^nu
nabla_mu T_support^{mu nu} = J_support^nu
```

with angular support exchange absent on the audited surface. This is the right
shape for a regulated anisotropic heat-current endpoint medium entrained to a
radial director, with a localized support reservoir restoring total
conservation.

The rapidity/source law is:

```text

h_reg = 1 - v_q^2 >= 0
S_psi = kappa_phase(s,l,stage,region) B_service(s,t) R_psi
```

where `kappa_phase` is bounded by the cap-0.95 phase-local source law on the
scaled support-edge entry/catch slices.

## Interpretation

This rung moves the source story from "known families fit the tensor" to "the
lead known family can be written as a conservative open-system fixed-background
equation skeleton." The source law is not a scalar, not an ordinary closed
fluid, and not a generic many-field escape hatch. The present lead is a
regulated anisotropic heat-current medium with an entrained radial director and
a localized support reservoir carrying explicit power and radial-force
exchange.

The result also narrows the burden of proof. The endpoint tensor, director
constraints, bounded rapidity, source schedule, observed PDE robustness, and
support localization are coherent together. The remaining issue is not "does
any recognizable physics fit?" It is whether the support-reservoir equations
can be promoted from this fixed-background skeleton into a sharper
action-level or symmetric-hyperbolic derivation without losing the thin
support-edge margins.
