\noindent\textbf{Document purpose.} This document discloses a representative active-rail spacetime metric engineering architecture, a source-placement control structure, and the diagnostic acceptance criteria used to characterize the design. The disclosure is organized in a patent-style technical format and emphasizes system components, operating modes, metric components, source ledgers, component roles, and affirmative operating criteria for source-family realization.

\section{Abstract}

An active-rail spacetime metric engineering system is disclosed. The system includes a prepared standing support substrate, a protected live packet corridor, a support-contained carrying-flow field, a locked timing layer for catch/rematch, a radial support-edge softening layer, a clock-lapse cushion, a rail-stretch sector, a throat-capacity/angular jacket, localized support-shell control components, a compact pressure-aware handoff layer, an entry service gate, and a release-choreography layer. The architecture separates baseline infrastructure support from service-induced transport demand. A dimensionless service factor $V$ specifies the carrying-flow operating load applied to the reduced metric. In the representative operating embodiment at $V=5$, the system preserves packet causal safety using a matched-hold release law, finite smooth beta fade, coordinated lapse/carve support, a blended trailing-edge packet beta-rematch sleeve, a two-feature packet-local radial support profile, and compact entry/catch/handoff support windows. The radial support profile uses a mild filled core stretch, a narrow annular catch ring, and a weaker annular skirt in $\gamma_{ll}$. The entry service gate separates setup support from live packet scoring, while the compact handoff layer separates entry pressure support from broad catch/edge smoothing so that radial pressure, angular/current, and radial-null source channels are assigned to distinct geometric functions. The support-shell source-ledger family is an infrastructure and source-placement control knob. An edge-load operating embodiment at $V=10$ supplies a stress condition for the same architecture. The diagnostic architecture treats derivative-matched handoff smoothing, compact pressure-aware source routing, semilocal smeared-null evaluation, radial support placement, and separated live-packet versus infrastructure source accounting as central control principles for affirmative demanded-source behavior.

\section{Technical Field}

The disclosure relates to prescribed spacetime metric engineering, numerical relativity scaffolding, source-placement architectures, and controlled metric-service systems. More particularly, the disclosure relates to a rail-like spacetime metric in which prepared infrastructure bears broad geometric support while live service demand is routed through localized handoff windows and packet-adjacent control regions.

\section{Engineering Objective}

A practical spacetime metric engineering architecture benefits from explicit source placement and explicit service timing. The disclosed system assigns standing radial support, throat/angular support, clock-lapse safety, carrying-flow load, entry service gating, catch/rematch correction, packet release, support-shell timing, and reset operations to distinct service components. The engineering objective is to provide a reduced metric and source-placement process that:

\begin{enumerate}
\item separates prepared substrate support from live transport increments;
\item keeps the protected packet corridor causally safe throughout live service;
\item localizes small service-induced momentum demand into catch/rematch and support-shell handoff regions;
\item expresses the reduced metric in ADM variables for constraint-ledger diagnosis;
\item subtracts the standing substrate from live service fields to isolate transport demand;
\item characterizes 4D demanded-source responses for load-bearing component assignment;
\item supports a transition from prescribed reduced geometry to coupled metric-family realization and coarse 3+1 constraint accounting.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Overview of the Active-Rail System}

The active rail operates as a coordinated metric service. A prepared standing substrate supplies the baseline throat, support shell, angular jacket, rail stretch, and clock-lapse structure. A live packet corridor lies inside the support shell. A carrying-flow field expresses the commanded service load through the prepared support infrastructure. The entry service gate assigns setup support to infrastructure accounting and begins live packet scoring inside the supported entry corridor. A locked timing layer coordinates support-side catch with packet rematch. A release-choreography layer then holds the packet briefly in a matched state, fades beta through a finite smooth profile, keeps lapse/carve support active through the handoff, and relaxes infrastructure support after the packet edge transfers the principal mismatch into the designated handoff windows. Packet-edge and support-shell control components are applied during catch/rematch and release to address localized handoff demand.

The invention-level structure is the componentized service architecture. The service factor $V$ is an operating-load parameter used to rate embodiments, select support/cushion/timing settings, and compare source-demand scaling. The primary operating embodiment is $V=5$. Lower-$V$ cases, such as $V=2$, provide diagnostic microscopes for mechanism and margin. The $V=10$ case supplies an edge-load embodiment for the same architecture. The representative V5 family is packet-safe under smoothing and grid refinement. Demanded-source burden is structured: live angular/current peaks are assigned to packet-in-support entry rows, while non-live radial-null peaks are concentrated in catch/rematch core-throat and support-edge infrastructure. The compact handoff layer reduces broad semilocal radial-null and angular-pressure concentration by using endpoint-smooth entry, catch, and edge windows, while pressure-aware entry shaping preserves a clear control channel for live radial pressure.

\section{Operating Regime and Role of \texorpdfstring{$V$}{V}}

The service factor $V$ is the operating rating assigned to the carrying-flow sector of the reduced active-rail metric. It is a natural focus parameter because one scalar sets the plateau level of the scheduled carrying-flow service before the architecture distributes that service through support-side catch, packet rematch, radial support, and release/fade envelopes. Holding the geometric support parameters fixed while varying $V$ therefore tests how a selected rail architecture responds to a higher or lower commanded carrying-flow load.

The scheduled support-side carrying-flow factor and the packet-frame comparison factor are obtained by blending $V$ with an exit or residual service factor $v_{\mathrm{exit}}$ through the locked timing schedules:
\begin{align}
U_{\beta}(\sig) &= v_{\mathrm{exit}}+\left(V-v_{\mathrm{exit}}\right)C_{\beta}(\sig),\\
U_{\mathrm{pkt}}(\sig) &= v_{\mathrm{exit}}+\left(V-v_{\mathrm{exit}}\right)C_{\mathrm{pkt}}(\sig),
\end{align}
where $C_{\beta}$ is the support-side catch schedule and $C_{\mathrm{pkt}}$ is the packet rematch schedule. In the locked-timing setting, $C_{\beta}$ begins with a controlled support-side lead while $C_{\mathrm{pkt}}$ is closely coupled by packet-norm safety. Thus $V$ selects the service level, while the choreography determines where and when that service level is expressed.

The carrying-flow factor enters the representative metric through the carrying-flow field $\beta$. A compact form of the support-contained carrying-flow ansatz is
\begin{equation}
\beta(l,\sig)=
-\,\frac{U_{\beta}(\sig)\,E(\sig)\,W(l)^{p_{\beta}}\,S(l,\sig)}{\Gthroat(l,\sig)},
\label{eq:v_beta_ansatz}
\end{equation}
where $E$ is the release/fade envelope, $W$ is the support-shell radial window, $S$ is the packet-adjacent service envelope, and $\Gthroat=\gamma_{\Omega\Omega}$ is the throat-capacity field. The packet-frame comparison uses
\begin{equation}
u_{\mathrm{pkt}}^{\,l}(\sig,l)=\frac{U_{\mathrm{pkt}}(\sig)}{\Gthroat(l,\sig)},
\qquad
\Npkt
=-\alpha^2+\Grail\left(u_{\mathrm{pkt}}^{\,l}+\beta\right)^2,
\label{eq:v_packet_norm}
\end{equation}
with packet causal safety requiring $\Npkt<0$ on the live packet corridor. In this sense, $V$ drives the metric service along the path
\begin{equation}
V \rightarrow U_{\beta},U_{\mathrm{pkt}}
\rightarrow \beta,\Npkt
\rightarrow K_{ij},\rho_{\mathrm{ADM}},j_l ,
\end{equation}
so a $V$-sweep probes the same architecture's carried-flow load, packet causal margin, and ADM source-placement response.

In representative diagnostics, $V=5$ is the primary operating embodiment. It provides a clean platform for ADM translation, standing-substrate subtraction, catch/rematch source-law characterization, release choreography, compact handoff shaping, and source-family accounting. Lower service factors, including $V=2$, inspect mechanism and margin. The $V=10$ embodiment exercises the same architecture at an edge-load rating. The representative V5 operating embodiment is characterized on V5 packet safety and source placement, with V10 retained as a separate edge-load stress rating.

\section{Representative Reduced Metric}

A representative reduced metric is expressed as
\begin{equation}
\dd s^2 = -\alpha(l,\sig)^2\dd\sig^2
+ \Grail(l,\sig)\left(\dd l+\beta(l,\sig)\dd\sig\right)^2
+ \Gthroat(l,\sig)\dd\Omega^2,
\label{eq:metric}
\end{equation}
where $l$ is the rail coordinate and $\sig$ is the service scheduling coordinate. The shift sector is intentionally retained in the completed-square form. This presentation displays the active-rail geometry as a Morris--Thorne-like intrinsic throat/support geometry, represented by $\Grail$ and $\Gthroat$, with a shift-like carrying-flow sector $\beta$ applied along the rail coordinate. The four metric-side service fields are:
\begin{align}
\alpha(l,\sig) &\quad\text{clock-lapse field},\\
\beta(l,\sig) &\quad\text{carrying-flow field},\\
\Grail(l,\sig)=\gamma_{ll} &\quad\text{rail-stretch field},\\
\Gthroat(l,\sig)=\gamma_{\Omega\Omega} &\quad\text{throat-capacity field}.
\end{align}
This notation reserves distinct symbols for metric components and support-shell overlay amplitudes.

\subsection{General Component Form}

The representative metric family can be described as a shaped baseline plus localized support-shell partners:
\begin{align}
\beta(l,\sig)&=\beta_0(l,\sig)+a_\beta \Wsh(l,\sig),\\
\alpha(l,\sig)&=\alpha_0(l,\sig)\exp\!\left(\kappa_\alpha \Wsh(l,\sig)\right),\\
\Grail(l,\sig)&=\Grail_0(l,\sig)\exp\!\left(\kappa_G \Wsh(l,\sig)\right),\\
\Gthroat(l,\sig)&=\Gthroat_0(l,\sig)\exp\!\left(\kappa_Q \Wsh(l,\sig)\right).
\label{eq:component_family}
\end{align}
The baseline fields $\alpha_0,\beta_0,\Grail_0,\Gthroat_0$ include the prepared throat, angular jacket, radial support-edge softening, lapse cushion, entry service gate, shaped catch/rematch timing, service release, packet-edge beta rematch, packet-local radial support shaping, compact entry/catch/handoff support, and packet-frame comparison structure. The localized partners are support-shell metric overlays. The continuous partner family includes carrying-flow ($a_\beta$), clock-lapse ($\kappa_\alpha$), rail-stretch ($\kappa_G$), and throat-capacity ($\kappa_Q$). Support-shell source-ledger accounting assigns carrying-flow plus clock-lapse as the default partner pair, rail-stretch as a peak-shaping comparator, and same-window throat-capacity as neutral in the default setting. The representative V5 operating embodiment uses release choreography, a blended trailing-edge packet beta-rematch sleeve, a two-feature packet-local radial support profile, and compact pressure-aware handoff support to preserve live packet safety and control demanded-source placement through the catch/release handoff.

The support-shell window is an annular, timed, packet-excluding window:
\begin{equation}
\Wsh(l,\sig)=R_{\mathrm{sh}}(|l|)\,
T_{\mathrm{sh}}(\sig;\lambda_c,\tau_c)\,
P_{\mathrm{excl}}(l,\sig),
\label{eq:wsh}
\end{equation}
where $R_{\mathrm{sh}}$ selects support-edge infrastructure, $T_{\mathrm{sh}}$ places the control action relative to catch/rematch, and $P_{\mathrm{excl}}$ suppresses direct live-packet overlap. In the representative implementation,
\begin{equation}
0.65\,\Rth\leq |l|\leq 1.20\,\Rth
\end{equation}
is the nominal support-shell band, $\lambda_c$ is the support-shell catch lead, and $\tau_c$ is the temporal width of the support-shell timing envelope.

The preferred V5 support-shell source-ledger comparator uses a compact raised-cosine annulus centered in the nominal support-shell band:
\begin{align}
R_{\mathrm{sh}}(r)
&=\frac{1}{2}\left[1+\cos\!\left(\pi\frac{|r-r_c|}{h_r}\right)\right],
\qquad |r-r_c|\leq h_r,\\
R_{\mathrm{sh}}(r)&=0,\qquad |r-r_c|>h_r,
\end{align}
where $r=|l|$, $r_c$ is the midpoint of the active support-shell band, and the preferred half-width is approximately $h_r=0.48125$ for $\Rth=1.75$ with the above multipliers. Gaussian and smooth-box annular windows are retained as comparator shapes, and the raised-cosine annulus gives the cleanest source-ledger behavior in matched-strength V5 accounting.

The preferred timing envelope is Gaussian in the service coordinate and normalized against the active catch/rematch edge:
\begin{equation}
T_{\mathrm{sh}}(\sig;\lambda_c,\tau_c)
=
\frac{\exp[-(\sig-\sig_c)^2/(2\tau_c^2)]}
{\max\left(\exp[-(\sig_\ast-\sig_c)^2/(2\tau_c^2)],\epsilon_T\right)},
\qquad
\sig_c=\sig_{\mathrm{catch}}-\lambda_c,
\end{equation}
where $\sig_\ast$ is the closest point in the active catch/rematch band to $\sig_c$. Minimum-jerk and raised-cosine temporal pulses are useful comparators. The representative timing band is $\tau_c=0.30$--$0.35$ with $\lambda_c=1.45$--$1.55$ for the V5 support-shell source-ledger family.

For matched-strength shape comparison, the support-shell carrying-flow overlay is parameterized by the peak shell perturbation
\begin{equation}
\Delta\beta_{\mathrm{sh,max}}=
\max_{l,\sig}\left|a_\beta\Wsh(l,\sig)\right|.
\end{equation}
This scalar makes radial and temporal shapes comparable at equal effective peak support-shell strength. The preferred V5 operating band is $\Delta\beta_{\mathrm{sh,max}}\approx0.20$--$0.25$, with $\Delta\beta_{\mathrm{sh,max}}\approx0.30$--$0.35$ used as an edge-stress range for point-peak and shell-throat concentration checks.

\subsection{Release Choreography, Compact Handoff, and Packet-Edge Rematch}

The representative V5 operating embodiment adds a release-choreography layer to the support-shell infrastructure. The release law is a coordinated handoff in which the packet is first caught/rematched, briefly held in a matched state, released through a finite smooth beta fade, and protected by lapse/carve support until packet-edge mismatch has been transferred into the designated handoff windows.

A representative release envelope is written as
\begin{equation}
\Wrel(\sig)=\mathcal{S}_5\!\left(
\frac{\sig-\sig_{\mathrm{hold}}}{\Delta\sig_{\beta}}
\right),
\qquad
\mathcal{S}_5(x)=10x^3-15x^4+6x^5,\quad 0\leq x\leq 1,
\end{equation}
with constant matched hold before the finite fade and saturated values outside the fade interval. Higher-smoothness profiles remain comparator choices, but the present preferred release setting uses a minimum-jerk profile because its endpoint derivatives vanish and because it keeps the release law interpretable.

The compact handoff layer expresses the same release principle spatially. It uses separate endpoint-smooth windows for entry containment, catch/rematch bearing, and trailing-edge handoff smoothing:
\begin{equation}
\Wch(l,\sig)=
g_e W_e(l,\sig)+g_c W_c(l,\sig)+g_h W_h(l,\sig),
\end{equation}
where $W_e$ supplies broad live-entry pressure containment, $W_c$ supplies catch/rematch bearing, and $W_h$ supplies outer packet-edge derivative smoothing. A representative high-resolution V5 compact handoff uses a seventh-order smoothstep transition, an entry containment strength near $0.75$, catch strength near $0.15$, edge strength near $0.16$, a broad edge width multiplier near $7.2$, and an entry service gate beginning near $\sig=-1.40$. This structure gives explicit control over radial pressure through the entry window while assigning angular/current and radial-null derivative relief to the catch and edge windows.

The entry service gate is a source-accounting boundary rather than a deletion of setup physics. Metric support before the gate is represented in the full infrastructure ledger, and live packet scoring begins once the packet has entered the supported corridor. In the representative high-resolution V5 ledger on the extended $101\times151$ domain, the entry-gated compact handoff has zero positive live packet-norm samples and a maximum live packet norm of approximately $-1.2424$.

The packet-edge beta rematch acts on the local mismatch
\begin{equation}
v_{\mathrm{coord}}+\beta_{\mathrm{pre}},
\end{equation}
where $\beta_{\mathrm{pre}}$ denotes the carrying-flow field before packet-local beta rematch. A compact notation for the packet-local correction is
\begin{equation}
\delta\beta_{\mathrm{pkt}}(l,\sig)
=-g_{\beta}\,\Wbeta(l,\sig)\,
\left(v_{\mathrm{coord}}+\beta_{\mathrm{pre}}(l,\sig)\right).
\end{equation}
The preferred $\Wbeta$ is a trailing-edge sleeve: weak in the packet center, strongest near the inner/behind side of the packet edge, and temporally active through the live catch/rematch corridor. The shaped sleeve is combined with a weak filled-core floor using a smooth blended union,
\begin{equation}
\Wbeta=W_{\mathrm{sleeve}}+W_{\mathrm{floor}}\left(1-W_{\mathrm{sleeve}}\right),
\end{equation}
which gives a single continuous packet-edge correction profile. The high-resolution V5 operating embodiment uses a beta-rematch gain of about $1.8$, center floor $0.60$, inner/outer sleeve radii approximately $0.75\,\Rpass$ and $1.10\,\Rpass$, and widened radial transition width multiplier $1.6$.

The blended floor is paired with sufficient trailing-edge sleeve width. In the V5 operating family, the wider sleeve preserves packet gates under refinement and substantially lowers point peaks. The compact handoff layer then broadens the same principle across entry, catch, and release by assigning pressure containment and derivative smoothing to separate smooth windows.

\subsection{Packet-Local Radial Support Profile}

The packet-local radial support profile modifies $\Grail=\gamma_{ll}$ through a smooth multiplicative factor:
\begin{equation}
\Grail(l,\sig)
=\Grail_{\mathrm{pre}}(l,\sig)
\exp\!\left(
g_c W_c(l,\sig)+g_r W_r(l,\sig)+g_s W_s(l,\sig)
\right),
\end{equation}
where $\Grail_{\mathrm{pre}}$ denotes the radial metric before packet-local radial support, $W_c$ is a filled packet/support core window, $W_r$ is a narrow annular catch ring, and $W_s$ is a weaker annular skirt. In the representative V5 setting,
\begin{align}
g_c&=+0.05,\qquad W_c:\ \text{entry/catch/release schedule, radius }1.3\Rpass,\ \text{width }1.8\wpass,\\
g_r&=+0.12,\qquad W_r:\ \text{catch-only annulus, outer radius }2.4\Rpass,\ \text{width }2.4\wpass,\\
g_s&=+0.05,\qquad W_s:\ \text{catch-only annulus, inner/outer radii }2.4\Rpass,\,2.6\Rpass,\ \text{width }3.2\wpass.
\end{align}
The core/ring/skirt structure separates two radial support functions. The narrow ring provides the principal radial-null and angular-pressure localization benefit. The weak skirt provides a smaller smoothing correction to live $p_l$ and $p_\Omega$ while preserving the main point-peak relief. High-resolution tests show that this two-feature radial profile preserves zero live packet-norm failures and lowers the radial-null point peak and angular-pressure burden relative to the release-only comparison family.

\section{ADM Translation}

The metric of Eq.~\eqref{eq:metric} is expressed in ADM form through
\begin{equation}
\gamma_{ij}=\mathrm{diag}\left(\Grail,\Gthroat,\Gthroat\sin^2\theta\right),\qquad
\beta^i=(\beta,0,0),\qquad
\beta_i=(\Grail\beta,0,0).
\end{equation}
The unit normal to a $\sig=\mathrm{constant}$ slice is
\begin{equation}
n^\mu=\left(\frac{1}{\alpha},-\frac{\beta}{\alpha},0,0\right),
\qquad n_\mu=(-\alpha,0,0,0).
\end{equation}

Using dot notation for $\partial_\sig$ and prime notation for $\partial_l$, and adopting the convention
\begin{equation}
K_{ij}=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(D_i\beta_j+D_j\beta_i-\partial_\sig\gamma_{ij}\right),
\end{equation}
the independent extrinsic-curvature components are
\begin{align}
K_{ll}&=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(2\Grail\beta'+\Grail'\beta-\dot{\Grail}\right),\\
K_{\theta\theta}&=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(\beta\Gthroat'-\dot{\Gthroat}\right),\\
K_{\phi\phi}&=K_{\theta\theta}\sin^2\theta.
\end{align}
The mixed eigenvalue-like quantities are
\begin{align}
k_l&=K^l_{\ l}=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(2\beta'+\beta\frac{\Grail'}{\Grail}-\frac{\dot{\Grail}}{\Grail}\right),\\
k_\Omega&=K^\theta_{\ \theta}=K^\phi_{\ \phi}
=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(\beta\frac{\Gthroat'}{\Gthroat}-\frac{\dot{\Gthroat}}{\Gthroat}\right).
\end{align}
Thus
\begin{equation}
K=k_l+2k_\Omega,
\qquad
K_{ij}K^{ij}=k_l^2+2k_\Omega^2.
\end{equation}
For spatial line element $\dd l_3^2=\Grail\dd l^2+\Gthroat\dd\Omega^2$, the scalar curvature is
\begin{equation}
{}^{(3)}R
= -\frac{2\Gthroat''}{\Grail\Gthroat}
+\frac{(\Gthroat')^2}{2\Grail\Gthroat^2}
+\frac{\Grail'\Gthroat'}{\Grail^2\Gthroat}
+\frac{2}{\Gthroat}.
\end{equation}
The Hamiltonian and radial momentum constraints produce the ADM ledger fields
\begin{align}
16\pi\rhoADM &= {}^{(3)}R+K^2-K_{ij}K^{ij}
= {}^{(3)}R+4k_lk_\Omega+2k_\Omega^2,\\
8\pi j_l&=-2k_\Omega'+\frac{\Gthroat'}{\Gthroat}(k_l-k_\Omega),\\
j_\theta&=0,\qquad j_\phi=0.
\end{align}

\section{Shell-Throat Correspondence Diagnostic}

Garattini and Zatrimaylov describe a wormhole--warp-drive correspondence in which a Morris--Thorne wormhole metric can be written in a warp-drive-like form by allowing nonzero intrinsic spatial curvature in the warp-drive metric \cite{GarattiniZatrimaylov2024}. In their preprint, the problematic layer is the shell region where a warp interpolation function overlaps the wormhole throat geometry. The relevant algebra contains mixed shell/throat terms: the warp interpolation profile, the shift-like sector, the wormhole intrinsic metric factor, and derivatives of the intrinsic metric factor all enter the same curvature expressions.

The active-rail support-shell results show an apparent structural intersection with that mechanism. In the present notation, the support-shell timing window $\Wsh$ plays the role of the localized shell/interpolation profile, $\beta$ is the carrying-flow or shift-like sector, and $\Grail,\Gthroat$ are the intrinsic support/throat metric components. A single-channel overlay
\begin{equation}
\beta=\beta_0+a_\beta\Wsh
\end{equation}
therefore supplies a shell-like carrying-flow feature while assigning the intrinsic metric response to the $\Grail$ and $\Gthroat$ partners. The demanded-source ledger then detects burden where $\Wsh$ overlaps gradients in $\Grail$ and $\Gthroat$.

This interpretation is supported by the shell-throat overlap probe. At $V=5$, $a_\beta=+0.5$, and $\tau_c=0.35$, a deliberately mistimed carrying-flow-only case with $\lambda_c=0.5$ raises the global radial-null burden ratio to about $1.181757$ and the shell-throat weighted radial-null ratio to about $3.054431$. With refined timing, the corresponding carrying-flow-only ratios fall substantially. The raised-cosine annulus improves the same structural layer: at matched peak support-shell strength, it reduces the main burden channels and drives live packet-norm drift to the numerical quiet level. Across the support-shell probe families, added radial-current and angular-pressure burden concentrate in the shell-throat overlap band when timing, radial shape, or intrinsic partner timing is mismatched.

The Garattini--Zatrimaylov correspondence identifies a conditional shell-throat concentration mechanism for the active rail. In the active-rail data, that mechanism is expressed when timing or metric coupling lets the support-shell carrying-flow overlay behave like a separable shell crossing an already-shaped support/throat geometry. In that decoupled regime, the source ledger assigns cost to the same structural layer highlighted by Garattini and Zatrimaylov: shell interpolation meeting intrinsic throat curvature. Refined support timing, clock-lapse coupling, and a smooth localized radial bearing shell place the design in a coordinated metric family. Shell-throat concentration is a distinct timing and gradient-matching diagnostic.

The algebraic-intersection diagnostic isolates the mixed terms. Matched controls flatten or smooth the support/throat gradients while keeping $\Wsh$, keep the throat/support geometry while broadening or shifting $\Wsh$, and compare carrying-flow-only, clock-lapse-only, rail-stretch-only, and throat-capacity-only cases. A proof-grade intersection shows that the measured $\Tkk$, $j_l$, and $p_\Omega$ deltas collapse when either the shell-gradient factor or the intrinsic throat-gradient factor is removed, and that a mixed-term proxy built from derivatives of $\Wsh$, $\beta$, $\Grail$, and $\Gthroat$ spatially correlates with the demanded-source delta maps.
