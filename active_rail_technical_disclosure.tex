\noindent\textbf{Document purpose.} This document discloses an active-rail spacetime metric engineering architecture, a source-placement control structure, and a validation program for selecting load-bearing metric families. The disclosure is organized in a patent-style technical format and emphasizes system components, operating modes, metric components, diagnostic procedures, acceptance criteria, validated prototype layers, and development gates for stronger source-realization work.

\section{Abstract}

An active-rail spacetime metric engineering system is disclosed. The system includes a prepared standing support substrate, a protected live packet corridor, a support-contained carrying-flow field, a locked timing layer for catch/rematch, a radial support-edge softening layer, a clock-lapse cushion, a rail-stretch sector, a throat-capacity/angular jacket, and localized support-shell control components. The architecture separates baseline infrastructure support from service-induced transport demand. A dimensionless service factor $V$ specifies the carrying-flow operating load applied to the reduced metric. In a representative operating embodiment at $V=5$, the system preserves packet causal safety and localizes service-induced momentum increments primarily into catch/rematch and support-shell infrastructure. A selected support-shell-bearing metric family uses a smooth localized radial bearing shell, Gaussian catch/rematch timing, matched peak carrying-flow strength, and clock-lapse coupling. An edge-load operating embodiment at $V=10$ exercises the same architecture as a stress condition. Prototype results identify gradient-matched support-shell shaping as the central control principle for cleaner 4D demanded-source behavior.

\section{Technical Field}

The disclosure relates to prescribed spacetime metric engineering, numerical relativity scaffolding, source-placement architectures, and controlled metric-service systems. More particularly, the disclosure relates to a staged rail-like spacetime metric in which prepared infrastructure bears broad geometric support while live service demand is routed through localized handoff windows and packet-adjacent control regions.

\section{Engineering Objective}

A practical spacetime metric engineering architecture benefits from explicit source placement and explicit service timing. The disclosed system assigns standing radial support, throat/angular support, clock-lapse safety, carrying-flow load, catch/rematch correction, packet release, support-shell timing, and reset operations to distinct service components. The engineering objective is to provide a reduced metric and prototype process that:

\begin{enumerate}
\item separates prepared substrate support from live transport increments;
\item keeps the protected packet corridor causally safe throughout live service;
\item localizes small service-induced momentum demand into catch/rematch and support-shell handoff regions;
\item expresses the reduced metric in ADM variables for constraint-ledger diagnosis;
\item subtracts the standing substrate from live service fields to isolate transport demand;
\item tests 4D demanded-source responses before promoting any component as load-bearing;
\item supports a staged transition from prescribed reduced geometry to coupled metric-family validation and a coarse 3+1 constraint prototype.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Overview of the Active-Rail System}

The active rail operates as a staged metric service. A prepared standing substrate supplies the baseline throat, support shell, angular jacket, rail stretch, and clock-lapse structure. A live packet corridor lies inside the support shell. A carrying-flow field expresses the commanded service load through the prepared support infrastructure. A locked timing layer coordinates support-side catch with packet rematch and returns the packet toward ordinary local geometry. Packet-edge and support-shell control components are applied during catch/rematch to address localized handoff demand.

The invention-level structure is the componentized service architecture. The service factor $V$ is an operating-load parameter used to rate embodiments, select support/cushion/timing settings, and compare source-demand scaling. The selected V5 support-shell branch is packet-safe and controllable as a continuous metric family. Its cleanest tested load-bearing behavior occurs when the support-shell radial bearing layer is smooth and localized, the timing is matched to catch/rematch, and the carrying-flow overlay is coupled to a clock-lapse partner.

\section{Operating Regime and Role of \texorpdfstring{$V$}{V}}

The service factor $V$ is the operating rating assigned to the carrying-flow sector of the reduced active-rail metric. It is a natural focus parameter because one scalar sets the plateau level of the scheduled carrying-flow service before the architecture distributes that service through support-side catch, packet rematch, radial support, and release/fade envelopes. Holding the geometric support parameters fixed while varying $V$ therefore tests how a selected rail architecture responds to a higher or lower commanded carrying-flow load.

The scheduled support-side carrying-flow factor and the packet-frame comparison factor are obtained by blending $V$ with an exit or residual service factor $v_{\mathrm{exit}}$ through the locked timing schedules:
\begin{align}
U_{\beta}(\sig) &= v_{\mathrm{exit}}+\left(V-v_{\mathrm{exit}}\right)C_{\beta}(\sig),\\
U_{\mathrm{pkt}}(\sig) &= v_{\mathrm{exit}}+\left(V-v_{\mathrm{exit}}\right)C_{\mathrm{pkt}}(\sig),
\end{align}
where $C_{\beta}$ is the support-side catch schedule and $C_{\mathrm{pkt}}$ is the packet rematch schedule. In the locked-timing branch, $C_{\beta}$ begins with a controlled support-side lead while $C_{\mathrm{pkt}}$ remains closely coupled by packet-norm safety. Thus $V$ selects the service level, while the choreography determines where and when that service level is expressed.

The carrying-flow factor enters the representative metric through the carrying-flow field $\beta$. A compact form of the support-contained carrying-flow ansatz is
\begin{equation}
\beta(l,\sig)=
-\,\frac{U_{\beta}(\sig)\,E(\sig)\,W(l)^{p_{\beta}}\,S(l,\sig)}{\Gthroat(l,\sig)},
\label{eq:v_beta_ansatz}
\end{equation}
where $E$ is the release/fade envelope, $W$ is the support-shell radial window, $S$ is the packet-adjacent service envelope, and $\Gthroat=\gamma_{\Omega\Omega}$ is the throat-capacity field. The packet-frame comparison uses
\begin{equation}
u_{\mathrm{pkt}}^{\,l}(\sig,l)=\frac{U_{\mathrm{pkt}}(\sig)}{\Gthroat(l,\sig)},
\qquad
\Npkt
=-\alpha^2+\Grail\left(u_{\mathrm{pkt}}^{\,l}+\beta\right)^2,
\label{eq:v_packet_norm}
\end{equation}
with packet causal safety requiring $\Npkt<0$ on the live packet corridor. In this sense, $V$ drives the metric service along the path
\begin{equation}
V \rightarrow U_{\beta},U_{\mathrm{pkt}}
\rightarrow \beta,\Npkt
\rightarrow K_{ij},\rho_{\mathrm{ADM}},j_l ,
\end{equation}
so a $V$-sweep probes the same architecture's carried-flow load, packet causal margin, and ADM source-placement response.

In representative diagnostics, $V=5$ provides the primary engineering target. It gives a clean platform for ADM translation, standing-substrate subtraction, catch/rematch source-law development, and source-family screening. The $V=10$ embodiment exercises the same architecture at an edge-load rating. The $V=10$ case is useful because it intensifies the catch/rematch handoff channel while preserving the same source-placement pattern observed at the primary target.

\section{Representative Reduced Metric}

A representative reduced metric is expressed as
\begin{equation}
\dd s^2 = -\alpha(l,\sig)^2\dd\sig^2
+ \Grail(l,\sig)\left(\dd l+\beta(l,\sig)\dd\sig\right)^2
+ \Gthroat(l,\sig)\dd\Omega^2,
\label{eq:metric}
\end{equation}
where $l$ is the rail coordinate and $\sig$ is the service scheduling coordinate. The shift sector is intentionally retained in the completed-square form. This presentation displays the active-rail geometry as a Morris--Thorne-like intrinsic throat/support geometry, represented by $\Grail$ and $\Gthroat$, with a shift-like carrying-flow sector $\beta$ applied along the rail coordinate. The four metric-side service fields are:
\begin{align}
\alpha(l,\sig) &\quad\text{clock-lapse field},\\
\beta(l,\sig) &\quad\text{carrying-flow field},\\
\Grail(l,\sig)=\gamma_{ll} &\quad\text{rail-stretch field},\\
\Gthroat(l,\sig)=\gamma_{\Omega\Omega} &\quad\text{throat-capacity field}.
\end{align}
This notation reserves distinct symbols for metric components and support-shell overlay amplitudes.

\subsection{General Component Form}

The present prototype family can be described as a shaped baseline plus localized support-shell partners:
\begin{align}
\beta(l,\sig)&=\beta_0(l,\sig)+a_\beta \Wsh(l,\sig),\\
\alpha(l,\sig)&=\alpha_0(l,\sig)\exp\!\left(\kappa_\alpha \Wsh(l,\sig)\right),\\
\Grail(l,\sig)&=\Grail_0(l,\sig)\exp\!\left(\kappa_G \Wsh(l,\sig)\right),\\
\Gthroat(l,\sig)&=\Gthroat_0(l,\sig)\exp\!\left(\kappa_Q \Wsh(l,\sig)\right).
\label{eq:component_family}
\end{align}
The baseline fields $\alpha_0,\beta_0,\Grail_0,\Gthroat_0$ include the prepared throat, angular jacket, radial support-edge softening, lapse cushion, shaped catch/rematch timing, service release, and packet-frame comparison structure. The localized partners are support-shell metric overlays. The continuous partner family includes carrying-flow ($a_\beta$), clock-lapse ($\kappa_\alpha$), rail-stretch ($\kappa_G$), and throat-capacity ($\kappa_Q$). The selected V5 branch uses carrying-flow and clock-lapse as active partners, keeps rail-stretch neutral by aggregate burden, and keeps same-window throat-capacity neutral in the default branch.

The support-shell window is an annular, timed, packet-excluding window:
\begin{equation}
\Wsh(l,\sig)=R_{\mathrm{sh}}(|l|)\,
T_{\mathrm{sh}}(\sig;\lambda_c,\tau_c)\,
P_{\mathrm{excl}}(l,\sig),
\label{eq:wsh}
\end{equation}
where $R_{\mathrm{sh}}$ selects support-edge infrastructure, $T_{\mathrm{sh}}$ places the control action relative to catch/rematch, and $P_{\mathrm{excl}}$ suppresses direct live-packet overlap. In the current implementation,
\begin{equation}
0.65\,\Rth\leq |l|\leq 1.20\,\Rth
\end{equation}
is the nominal support-shell band, $\lambda_c$ is the support-shell catch lead, and $\tau_c$ is the temporal width of the support-shell timing envelope.

The selected V5 support-shell radial bearing function is a compact raised-cosine annulus centered in the nominal support-shell band:
\begin{align}
R_{\mathrm{sh}}(r)
&=\frac{1}{2}\left[1+\cos\!\left(\pi\frac{|r-r_c|}{h_r}\right)\right],
\qquad |r-r_c|\leq h_r,\\
R_{\mathrm{sh}}(r)&=0,\qquad |r-r_c|>h_r,
\end{align}
where $r=|l|$, $r_c$ is the midpoint of the active support-shell band, and the preferred half-width is approximately $h_r=0.48125$ for $\Rth=1.75$ with the above multipliers. Gaussian and smooth-box annular windows remain comparator shapes, but the raised-cosine annulus gives the cleanest source-ledger behavior in the matched-strength V5 tests.

The preferred timing envelope is Gaussian in the service coordinate and normalized against the active catch/rematch edge:
\begin{equation}
T_{\mathrm{sh}}(\sig;\lambda_c,\tau_c)
=
\frac{\exp[-(\sig-\sig_c)^2/(2\tau_c^2)]}
{\max\left(\exp[-(\sig_\ast-\sig_c)^2/(2\tau_c^2)],\epsilon_T\right)},
\qquad
\sig_c=\sig_{\mathrm{catch}}-\lambda_c,
\end{equation}
where $\sig_\ast$ is the closest point in the active catch/rematch band to $\sig_c$. Minimum-jerk and raised-cosine temporal pulses remain useful comparators. The stable tested timing band is $\tau_c=0.30$--$0.35$ with $\lambda_c=1.45$--$1.55$ for the selected V5 family.

For matched-strength shape comparison, the support-shell carrying-flow overlay is parameterized by the peak shell perturbation
\begin{equation}
\Delta\beta_{\mathrm{sh,max}}=
\max_{l,\sig}\left|a_\beta\Wsh(l,\sig)\right|.
\end{equation}
This scalar makes radial and temporal shapes comparable at equal effective peak support-shell strength. The preferred V5 operating band is $\Delta\beta_{\mathrm{sh,max}}\approx0.20$--$0.25$, with $\Delta\beta_{\mathrm{sh,max}}\approx0.30$--$0.35$ retained as an edge-stress range for point-peak and shell-throat concentration checks.

\section{ADM Translation}

The metric of Eq.~\eqref{eq:metric} is expressed in ADM form through
\begin{equation}
\gamma_{ij}=\mathrm{diag}\left(\Grail,\Gthroat,\Gthroat\sin^2\theta\right),\qquad
\beta^i=(\beta,0,0),\qquad
\beta_i=(\Grail\beta,0,0).
\end{equation}
The unit normal to a $\sig=\mathrm{constant}$ slice is
\begin{equation}
n^\mu=\left(\frac{1}{\alpha},-\frac{\beta}{\alpha},0,0\right),
\qquad n_\mu=(-\alpha,0,0,0).
\end{equation}

Using dot notation for $\partial_\sig$ and prime notation for $\partial_l$, and adopting the convention
\begin{equation}
K_{ij}=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(D_i\beta_j+D_j\beta_i-\partial_\sig\gamma_{ij}\right),
\end{equation}
the independent extrinsic-curvature components are
\begin{align}
K_{ll}&=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(2\Grail\beta'+\Grail'\beta-\dot{\Grail}\right),\\
K_{\theta\theta}&=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(\beta\Gthroat'-\dot{\Gthroat}\right),\\
K_{\phi\phi}&=K_{\theta\theta}\sin^2\theta.
\end{align}
The mixed eigenvalue-like quantities are
\begin{align}
k_l&=K^l_{\ l}=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(2\beta'+\beta\frac{\Grail'}{\Grail}-\frac{\dot{\Grail}}{\Grail}\right),\\
k_\Omega&=K^\theta_{\ \theta}=K^\phi_{\ \phi}
=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(\beta\frac{\Gthroat'}{\Gthroat}-\frac{\dot{\Gthroat}}{\Gthroat}\right).
\end{align}
Thus
\begin{equation}
K=k_l+2k_\Omega,
\qquad
K_{ij}K^{ij}=k_l^2+2k_\Omega^2.
\end{equation}
For spatial line element $\dd l_3^2=\Grail\dd l^2+\Gthroat\dd\Omega^2$, the scalar curvature is
\begin{equation}
{}^{(3)}R
= -\frac{2\Gthroat''}{\Grail\Gthroat}
+\frac{(\Gthroat')^2}{2\Grail\Gthroat^2}
+\frac{\Grail'\Gthroat'}{\Grail^2\Gthroat}
+\frac{2}{\Gthroat}.
\end{equation}
The Hamiltonian and radial momentum constraints produce the ADM ledger fields
\begin{align}
16\pi\rhoADM &= {}^{(3)}R+K^2-K_{ij}K^{ij}
= {}^{(3)}R+4k_lk_\Omega+2k_\Omega^2,\\
8\pi j_l&=-2k_\Omega'+\frac{\Gthroat'}{\Gthroat}(k_l-k_\Omega),\\
j_\theta&=0,\qquad j_\phi=0.
\end{align}

\section{Catch-Edge Control Law}

Let
\begin{equation}
\xi=l-s(\sig)
\end{equation}
be a packet-centered radial coordinate. A preferred absorber amplitude is a compact kinematic handoff law multiplied by an inner-edge envelope:
\begin{equation}
A_{\mathrm{catch}}(\xi,\sig)
= W_{\mathrm{catch}}(\sig)
\left[a\,H_{\mathrm{flow}}(\xi,\sig)+b\,B_{\mathrm{inner}}(\xi)\right],
\end{equation}
where $W_{\mathrm{catch}}$ is a locked timing window, $H_{\mathrm{flow}}$ is a smooth carrying-flow handoff or flow-mismatch measure, and $B_{\mathrm{inner}}$ is a compact bump centered near $\xi=-\Rpass$. A residual-responsive limiter may bound the amplitude:
\begin{equation}
A_{\mathrm{catch}}\leq C\left(\left[R_{\Tkk}\right]_+\right),
\end{equation}
where $C$ denotes a smooth cap and $R_{\Tkk}$ denotes the predicted radial-null residual. The primary control law is kinematic; the residual-responsive term supplies protective limiting and diagnostic alignment.

\section{Shell-Throat Correspondence Diagnostic}

Garattini and Zatrimaylov describe a wormhole--warp-drive correspondence in which a Morris--Thorne wormhole metric can be written in a warp-drive-like form by allowing nonzero intrinsic spatial curvature in the warp-drive metric \cite{GarattiniZatrimaylov2024}. In their preprint, the problematic layer is the shell region where a warp interpolation function overlaps the wormhole throat geometry. The relevant algebra contains mixed shell/throat terms: the warp interpolation profile, the shift-like sector, the wormhole intrinsic metric factor, and derivatives of the intrinsic metric factor all enter the same curvature expressions.

The active-rail support-shell results show an apparent structural intersection with that mechanism. In the present notation, the support-shell timing window $\Wsh$ plays the role of the localized shell/interpolation profile, $\beta$ is the carrying-flow or shift-like sector, and $\Grail,\Gthroat$ are the intrinsic support/throat metric components. A single-channel overlay
\begin{equation}
\beta=\beta_0+a_\beta\Wsh
\end{equation}
therefore supplies a shell-like carrying-flow feature while assigning the intrinsic metric response to the $\Grail$ and $\Gthroat$ partners. The demanded-source ledger then detects burden where $\Wsh$ overlaps gradients in $\Grail$ and $\Gthroat$.

This interpretation is supported by the shell-throat overlap probe. At $V=5$, $a_\beta=+0.5$, and $\tau_c=0.35$, a deliberately mistimed carrying-flow-only case with $\lambda_c=0.5$ raises the global radial-null burden ratio to about $1.181757$ and the shell-throat weighted radial-null ratio to about $3.054431$. With refined timing, the corresponding carrying-flow-only ratios fall substantially. The raised-cosine annulus improves the same structural layer: at matched peak support-shell strength, it reduces the main burden channels and drives live packet-norm drift to the numerical quiet level. Across the support-shell probe families, added radial-current and angular-pressure burden concentrate in the shell-throat overlap band when timing, radial shape, or intrinsic partner timing is mismatched.

The best present conclusion is that the Garattini--Zatrimaylov correspondence identifies a conditional failure-mode mechanism for the active rail. In the active-rail data, that mechanism lights up when timing or metric coupling lets the support-shell carrying-flow overlay behave like a separable shell crossing an already-shaped support/throat geometry. In that decoupled regime, the source ledger pays cost in the same structural layer highlighted by Garattini and Zatrimaylov: shell interpolation meeting intrinsic throat curvature. Refined support timing, clock-lapse coupling, and a smooth localized radial bearing shell move the design toward a single coordinated metric family. Shell-throat concentration remains a distinct timing and gradient-matching diagnostic.

The algebraic-intersection diagnostic isolates the mixed terms. The practical test is to run matched controls that flatten or smooth the support/throat gradients while keeping $\Wsh$, keep the throat/support geometry while broadening or shifting $\Wsh$, and compare carrying-flow-only, clock-lapse-only, rail-stretch-only, and throat-capacity-only variants. A proof-grade intersection would show that the measured $\Tkk$, $j_l$, and $p_\Omega$ deltas collapse when either the shell-gradient factor or the intrinsic throat-gradient factor is removed, and that a mixed-term proxy built from derivatives of $\Wsh$, $\beta$, $\Grail$, and $\Gthroat$ spatially correlates with the demanded-source delta maps.

\section{Risk Controls and Diagnostic Alarms}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Packet-region density increment.} A large $\Delta\rho$ concentration inside the protected packet would trigger packet-corridor redesign.
\item \textbf{Refinement-sharpening catch layer.} A catch-edge layer that sharpens under grid refinement would trigger absorber broadening or source-family rejection.
\item \textbf{Angular-pressure leakage.} Excess growth in $p_\Omega$ or angular support entering the packet region would trigger redesign of the throat-capacity/angular jacket sector.
\item \textbf{Momentum delocalization.} $\Delta j_l$ spreading across the packet interior would trigger revision of the handoff model.
\item \textbf{Single-channel additive burden.} Carrying-flow amplitude that adds radial-null or radial-current burden would trigger coupled clock-lapse, rail-stretch, or throat-capacity refinement.
\item \textbf{Shell-throat overlap concentration.} Added radial-null, radial-current, or angular-pressure burden concentrated where $\Wsh$ overlaps $\Grail,\Gthroat$ gradients would trigger timing broadening or intrinsic-metric partner refinement.
\item \textbf{Point-peak growth.} A source-ledger case with acceptable integrated burden but growing local peaks would trigger timing broadening, strength reduction, or source-family smoothing.
\item \textbf{Radial bearing over-broadening.} A support-shell annulus that spreads into packet-sensitive structure would trigger return to the localized raised-cosine bearing band or a narrower comparator family.
\item \textbf{Edge-load qualitative transition.} A $V=10$ source pattern with a category-level change would place the edge case outside the primary prototype rating.
\item \textbf{Constraint instability.} Growing constraint residuals in the coarse 3+1 scaffold would trigger smoothing, reparameterization, or source-ledger redesign.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Conclusion}

The active-rail system organizes spacetime metric engineering as a source-placement architecture. The primary $V=5$ operating embodiment separates standing substrate curvature from live service demand and localizes the selected small support-shell service increment into catch/rematch support infrastructure while preserving packet safety. The $V=10$ operating embodiment supplies an edge-condition comparison for the same architecture and confirms the survival of the selected small support-shell target.

The refined metric understanding is componentized: carrying-flow $\beta$ expresses scheduled service load, clock-lapse $\alpha$ protects timing and causal margin, rail-stretch $\Grail$ controls radial metric capacity, and throat-capacity $\Gthroat$ carries angular/throat support. The continuous support-shell overlay is written as a metric-side family. Current demanded-source results select a raised-cosine annular support-shell bearing layer, Gaussian timing, moderate matched peak shell strength, and clock-lapse coupling as the cleanest V5 support-shell branch.

The engineering path is therefore focused: run richer source-objective and conservation-style validation on the selected V5 basin, confirm the selected candidates at higher resolution, isolate the shell-throat mixed terms, assign source families to component roles, and then build the coarse 3+1 constraint prototype around the surviving coupled metric family.
