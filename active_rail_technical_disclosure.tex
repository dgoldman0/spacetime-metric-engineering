\noindent\textbf{Document purpose.} This document discloses an active-rail spacetime metric engineering architecture, a source-placement control structure, and an engineering prototype plan. The disclosure is organized in a patent-style technical format and emphasizes system components, operating modes, diagnostic procedures, acceptance criteria, and prototype implementation stages.

\section{Abstract}

An active-rail spacetime metric engineering system is disclosed. The system includes a prepared standing support substrate, a protected live packet corridor, a support-contained radial shift carrier, a locked-lead catch/rematch layer, a radial support-edge softening layer, a lapse-cushion sector, an angular support jacket, and a localized catch-edge absorber. The architecture separates baseline infrastructure support from service-induced transport demand. A dimensionless service factor $V$ specifies the operating load applied to the carried-shift sector of the reduced metric. In a representative operating embodiment at $V=5$, the system preserves packet causal safety and localizes the service-induced ADM momentum increment primarily into a packet-adjacent catch/rematch handoff channel. A high-stress operating embodiment at $V=10$ exercises the same architecture near its recognized edge rating. The prototype plan translates the reduced metric fields into ADM variables, computes constraint-ledger fields, subtracts standing substrate demand from live service demand, and screens source-family candidates against localized component roles.

\section{Technical Field}

The disclosure relates to prescribed spacetime metric engineering, numerical relativity scaffolding, source-placement architectures, and controlled metric-service systems. More particularly, the disclosure relates to a staged rail-like spacetime metric in which prepared infrastructure bears broad geometric support while live service demand is routed through localized handoff windows and packet-adjacent control regions.

\section{Engineering Objective}

A practical spacetime metric engineering architecture benefits from explicit source placement. The disclosed system assigns standing radial support, angular support, lapse safety, carried shift, catch/rematch correction, packet release, and reset operations to distinct service components. The engineering objective is to provide a reduced metric and prototype process that:

\begin{enumerate}
\item separates prepared substrate support from live transport increments;
\item keeps the protected packet corridor causally safe throughout live service;
\item localizes service-induced momentum demand into catch/rematch and support handoff regions;
\item expresses the reduced metric in ADM variables for constraint-ledger diagnosis;
\item subtracts the standing substrate from live service fields to isolate transport demand;
\item supports a staged transition from prescribed reduced geometry to a coarse 3+1 constraint prototype.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Overview of the Active-Rail System}

The active rail operates as a staged metric service. A prepared standing substrate supplies the baseline throat, support shell, angular jacket, and lapse structure. A live packet corridor lies inside the support shell. A radial shift field carries the service demand through the prepared support rather than through a broad passenger-facing shell. A locked-lead catch/rematch layer absorbs the carried shift and returns the packet toward ordinary local geometry. A compact absorber is applied near the inward packet edge during catch/rematch to address localized handoff demand.

The invention-level structure is the componentized service architecture. The service factor $V$ is an operating-load parameter used to rate embodiments, select support/cushion/absorber settings, and compare source-demand scaling. The core system remains the staged active rail: prepared support substrate, protected packet corridor, support-contained carried shift, locked-lead catch/rematch, packet-adjacent absorber, and resettable infrastructure.

\section{Operating Regime and Role of $V$}

The parameter $V$ is used as a design-rating parameter. Increasing $V$ increases the carried-shift service demand and exposes how the same support geometry responds to higher transport load. The parameter therefore functions as a controlled stress dial for the reduced architecture. It supports comparison across operating embodiments without making the invention depend on a single numerical target.

In representative diagnostics, $V=5$ provides the primary engineering target. It gives a clean platform for ADM translation, standing-substrate subtraction, catch/rematch source-law development, and source-family screening. The $V=10$ embodiment exercises the same architecture at a high-stress edge rating. The $V=10$ case is useful because it intensifies the catch/rematch handoff channel while preserving the same source-placement pattern observed at the primary target.

\section{Service Sequence}

A representative service cycle proceeds through the following phases:
\begin{equation}
\begin{array}{c}
\text{prepared substrate}\rightarrow\text{ready support plant}\rightarrow\text{packet entry}\rightarrow\text{locked-lead catch/rematch}\\
\rightarrow\text{support-contained shift handoff}\rightarrow\text{packet release}\rightarrow\text{infrastructure reset}.
\end{array}
\end{equation}

Passenger-facing accounting is assigned to packet entry, catch/rematch, carried service, release, and any selected live buffer window. Reset and decompression are infrastructure operations unless a diagnostic window intentionally includes them for system-level energy accounting.

\section{ADM Translation}

The metric of Eq.~\eqref{eq:metric} is expressed in ADM form through
\begin{equation}
\gamma_{ij}=\mathrm{diag}\left(A,B,B\sin^2\theta\right),\qquad
\beta^i=(\beta,0,0),\qquad
\beta_i=(A\beta,0,0).
\end{equation}
The unit normal to a $\sig=\mathrm{constant}$ slice is
\begin{equation}
n^\mu=\left(\frac{1}{\alpha},-\frac{\beta}{\alpha},0,0\right),
\qquad n_\mu=(-\alpha,0,0,0).
\end{equation}

Using dot notation for $\partial_\sig$ and prime notation for $\partial_l$, and adopting the convention
\begin{equation}
K_{ij}=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(D_i\beta_j+D_j\beta_i-\partial_\sig\gamma_{ij}\right),
\end{equation}
the independent extrinsic-curvature components are
\begin{align}
K_{ll}&=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(2A\beta'+A'\beta-\dot A\right),\\
K_{\theta\theta}&=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(\beta B'-\dot B\right),\\
K_{\phi\phi}&=K_{\theta\theta}\sin^2\theta.
\end{align}
The mixed eigenvalue-like quantities are
\begin{align}
k_l&=K^l_{\ l}=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(2\beta'+\beta\frac{A'}{A}-\frac{\dot A}{A}\right),\\
k_\Omega&=K^\theta_{\ \theta}=K^\phi_{\ \phi}
=\frac{1}{2\alpha}\left(\beta\frac{B'}{B}-\frac{\dot B}{B}\right).
\end{align}
Thus
\begin{equation}
K=k_l+2k_\Omega,
\qquad
K_{ij}K^{ij}=k_l^2+2k_\Omega^2.
\end{equation}
For spatial line element $\dd l_3^2=A\dd l^2+B\dd\Omega^2$, the scalar curvature is
\begin{equation}
{}^{(3)}R
= -\frac{2B''}{AB}
+\frac{(B')^2}{2AB^2}
+\frac{A'B'}{A^2B}
+\frac{2}{B}.
\end{equation}
The Hamiltonian and radial momentum constraints produce the ADM ledger fields
\begin{align}
16\pi\rhoADM &= {}^{(3)}R+K^2-K_{ij}K^{ij}
= {}^{(3)}R+4k_lk_\Omega+2k_\Omega^2,\\
8\pi j_l&=-2k_\Omega'+\frac{B'}{B}(k_l-k_\Omega),\\
j_\theta&=0,\qquad j_\phi=0.
\end{align}

\section{Catch-Edge Control Law}

Let
\begin{equation}
\xi=l-s(\sig)
\end{equation}
be a packet-centered radial coordinate. A preferred absorber amplitude is a compact kinematic handoff law multiplied by an inner-edge envelope:
\begin{equation}
A_{\mathrm{catch}}(\xi,\sig)
= W_{\mathrm{catch}}(\sig)
\left[a\,H_{\mathrm{shift}}(\xi,\sig)+b\,B_{\mathrm{inner}}(\xi)\right],
\end{equation}
where $W_{\mathrm{catch}}$ is a locked-lead timing window, $H_{\mathrm{shift}}$ is a smooth shift-handoff or shift-mismatch measure, and $B_{\mathrm{inner}}$ is a compact bump centered near $\xi=-\Rpass$. A residual-responsive limiter may bound the amplitude:
\begin{equation}
A_{\mathrm{catch}}\leq C\left(\left[R_{\Tkk}\right]_+\right),
\end{equation}
where $C$ denotes a smooth cap and $R_{\Tkk}$ denotes the predicted radial-null residual. The primary control law is kinematic; the residual-responsive term supplies protective limiting and diagnostic alignment.

\section{Standing Substrate Subtraction}

Standing substrate subtraction isolates service-induced ADM demand from prepared infrastructure demand. For a live service field $F_{\mathrm{live}}$ and a time-matched prepared beta-off substrate field $F_{\mathrm{sub}}$, define
\begin{align}
\delrho&=\rho_{\mathrm{ADM,live}}-\rho_{\mathrm{ADM,sub}},\\
\delj&=j_{l,\mathrm{live}}-j_{l,\mathrm{sub}}.
\end{align}
This diagnostic determines whether packet-region ADM activity belongs to baseline support curvature or to the active service increment.

\section{Prototype Plan}

\subsection{Prototype Objective}

The prototype objective is to construct a coarse ADM diagnostic scaffold for the $V=5$ active-rail service and determine whether the prescribed reduced metric produces a constraint-readable source-placement target. The prototype begins with prescribed geometry, then proceeds toward constraint-compatible source modeling.

\subsection{Stage 1: Exact Field Builder and ADM Ledger}

Inputs:
\begin{itemize}
\item exact field functions $\alpha(l,\sig)$, $\beta(l,\sig)$, $A(l,\sig)$, and $B(l,\sig)$;
\item packet, support, stage, and region labels;
\item primary $V=5$ operating target and high-stress $V=10$ comparison case.
\end{itemize}

Outputs:
\begin{itemize}
\item maps of $k_l$, $k_\Omega$, $K$, ${}^{(3)}R$, $\rhoADM$, and $j_l$;
\item region and stage burden tables;
\item packet norm and packet exposure diagnostics.
\end{itemize}

Acceptance criteria:
\begin{enumerate}
\item all live-packet norm samples remain negative;
\item ADM fields remain finite on the diagnostic grid;
\item live momentum burden concentrates in support/catch/rematch rather than packet interior;
\item live negative $\rhoADM$ packet burden remains zero or negligible.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Stage 2: Standing Substrate Subtraction}

Compute beta-off or ready-state substrate fields and subtract them from live service fields. The objective is to identify the service-induced $\Delta\rho$ and $\Delta j_l$ increments.

Acceptance criteria:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\Delta\rho$ remains small inside the live packet;
\item $\Delta j_l$ is localized near catch/rematch and support-side handoff regions;
\item the $V=10$ case increases handoff demand while preserving the qualitative localization pattern observed at $V=5$.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Stage 3: Compact Source-Control Law}

Replace fitted modal catch-edge components with a compact control law. The preferred embodiment uses a kinematic handoff amplitude and an inner-edge spatial envelope. The law is tested against residual maps and ADM $\Delta j_l$ maps.

Acceptance criteria:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the law captures the majority of catch/rematch $\Delta j_l$ demand at $V=5$;
\item the support remains localized near $\xi=-\Rpass$;
\item packet-region $\Delta\rho$ remains small;
\item scaling from $V=5$ toward $V=10$ remains smooth.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Stage 4: Coarse 3+1 Constraint Prototype}

A coarse 3+1 prototype imposes or solves partial constraint data using the ADM scaffold as the target. Candidate subtests include:
\begin{itemize}
\item fixing $\gamma_{ij}$ and computing compatible source ledgers;
\item smoothing or regularizing the catch-edge $\Delta j_l$ layer;
\item comparing beta-off, low-beta, and full-beta substrate choices;
\item testing convergence of $\Delta\rho$ and $\Delta j_l$ under grid refinement;
\item repeating the acceptance suite at $V=10$ as an edge-condition comparison.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Stage 5: Source-Family Screening}

Candidate source families are screened against the component ledger. Source families may be assigned to standing support, angular jacket, lapse support, shift carrier, and catch-edge absorber roles. Preference is given to source families that keep amplitudes bounded, preserve packet-region safety, and maintain localized support roles under refinement.

\section{Risk Controls and Diagnostic Alarms}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Packet-region density increment.} A large $\Delta\rho$ concentration inside the protected packet would trigger packet-corridor redesign.
\item \textbf{Refinement-sharpening catch layer.} A catch-edge layer that sharpens under grid refinement would trigger absorber broadening or source-family rejection.
\item \textbf{Angular jacket leakage.} Angular support entering the packet region would trigger redesign of the angular jacket or support-shell partition.
\item \textbf{Momentum delocalization.} $\Delta j_l$ spreading across the packet interior would trigger revision of the handoff model.
\item \textbf{High-stress qualitative transition.} A $V=10$ source pattern that changes category rather than magnitude would place the edge case outside the primary prototype rating.
\item \textbf{Constraint instability.} Growing constraint residuals in the coarse 3+1 scaffold would trigger smoothing, reparameterization, or source-ledger redesign.
\end{enumerate}
